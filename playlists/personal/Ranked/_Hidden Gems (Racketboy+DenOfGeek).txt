Hidden Gems (Racketboy+DenOfGeek)

This playlist gets interesting because it combines two different kinds of "hidden gem." Racketboy tends to champion games that were mechanically unusual, niche, or easy to miss the first time around. Den of Geek is more likely to rescue games that were well-liked but got buried under the same small canon of Zelda, Mario, Rare, and party staples. The overlap between those approaches is where the real discovery happens.

Seen that way, the N64's reputation for having a thin library starts to look incomplete. Once you move beyond the obvious all-timers, the system is full of ambitious side roads: Body Harvest's open-ended chaos, Space Station Silicon Valley's idea density, Mischief Makers' strange 2D energy, Rocket: Robot on Wheels' physics-driven play, Hybrid Heaven's genre mash-up, and Ogre Battle 64's stubbornly serious strategy scope.

Some of these games were never truly obscure; they were just crowded out by louder legends. Others are rough, eccentric, or proudly mid-budget. That is part of the appeal. A hidden-gems playlist should not just repeat consensus classics with lower sales numbers. It should show where the library gets weirder, riskier, and more personal.
