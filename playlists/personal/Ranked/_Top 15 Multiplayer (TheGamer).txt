Top 15 Multiplayer (TheGamer)

The Nintendo 64's multiplayer reputation is bigger than any one ranking because the hardware itself helped script how people remember the console. Four controller ports were not a side feature; they were a statement. The machine entered living rooms already arranged for shared play, and that changed what kinds of memories stuck to it. Plenty of consoles had great versus games. The N64 became the one people associated with yelling across a couch.

That is why a multiplayer playlist on this system cuts across genres so cleanly. TheGamer calls the N64 the multiplayer titan of its generation, and the claim holds up because the roster is not built on a single formula. You get kart racing, party games, wrestling systems, shooters, sports, and early platform fighting all competing for the same social space. GoldenEye and Perfect Dark taught people to live with split-screen espionage chaos. Mario Kart 64 and Mario Tennis made readability and trash talk part of the design. Smash turned Nintendo mascots into a house rules machine. AKI's wrestling games proved slower, more tactile systems could be even better in a room full of friends than flashy arcade spectacle.

What makes the N64 special is not just that these games were fun in multiplayer. It is that the console made multiplayer feel like the default use case for an entire era of domestic gaming. This playlist is best understood as social hardware history.
