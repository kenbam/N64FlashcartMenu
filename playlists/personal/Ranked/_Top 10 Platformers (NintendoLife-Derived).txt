Top 10 Platformers (NintendoLife-Derived)

The N64 platformer story is really the story of what happened after Super Mario 64 blew the doors off 3D movement. Once Nintendo established free analog movement, camera play, and open-feeling stages as the new standard, everybody else had to decide whether to follow it, elaborate on it, or dodge around it. That is why this playlist is historically useful: it shows several different answers to the same design problem.

Banjo-Kazooie and Banjo-Tooie turn Mario's movement breakthrough into a more collectible, talkative, densely stuffed style of 3D platforming. Donkey Kong 64 pushes that logic toward maximalism. Kirby 64 and Yoshi's Story show Nintendo also kept space open for softer, more guided platformers with stronger visual identity than pure mechanical freedom. Then there are the outliers: Mischief Makers stays mostly 2D and feels almost defiant about it, while Rocket: Robot on Wheels treats physics as a core toy rather than mere stage dressing.

That mix is why the N64 still matters to platformer history. It was not just the birthplace of one revolutionary game. It was a brief, messy lab where developers were trying to work out what 3D platforming would become, and several branches of that future are still visible here.
