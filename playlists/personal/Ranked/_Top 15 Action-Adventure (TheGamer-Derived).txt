Top 15 Action-Adventure (TheGamer-Derived)

On the N64, action-adventure quickly became the genre where the console's reputation narrowed around Zelda, even though the broader field was much stranger than that. Ocarina of Time and Majora's Mask cast such a long shadow that they can make the rest of the machine look tidier and more unified than it really was. A playlist like this is useful because it restores the mess.

Yes, Zelda sets the prestige standard. But once you move past it, the category starts sprawling in interesting directions. Banjo and Conker blur into platforming and exploration. Mystical Ninja and Goemon's Great Adventure carry a completely different tone, drawing on eccentric Japanese adventure comedy rather than western epic structure. Jet Force Gemini leans toward shooter territory, Paper Mario softens the category with RPG framing, and games like Body Harvest, Shadow Man, Castlevania, and Mega Man 64 reveal how experimental late-90s 3D adventure design still was.

So the real value of this playlist is not proving that Zelda was king. It is showing how many incompatible ideas could still coexist under the same broad label before modern action-adventure design settled into more standardized forms.
