Rare on N64

Rare's N64 run is one of the clearest cases of a single studio expanding what a console seems capable of. Nintendo established the machine with Mario and Zelda, but Rare kept widening the pitch: shooters, mascot platformers, kart racing, sci-fi action, destruction sandboxes, and some of the console's most durable multiplayer memories all came from the same house.

What makes this period special is range. GoldenEye 007 helped define console FPS design. Perfect Dark pushed that template into something denser and stranger. Banjo-Kazooie and Banjo-Tooie gave the system a warmer, more exploratory personality, while Blast Corps, Diddy Kong Racing, Jet Force Gemini, and Donkey Kong 64 showed that Rare could bounce between experiments and crowd-pleasers without losing its identity.

This playlist works best as a reminder that Rare was not just making hits; it was helping write the N64's cultural memory. For many players, the console's personality is inseparable from Rare's mix of technical ambition, odd humor, collectathon excess, and couch-multiplayer chaos.
